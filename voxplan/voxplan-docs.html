Installation

VoxPlan is available as a direct APK download for Android devices. Follow these steps to install:

Requirements

  • Android device running Android 8.1 (API level 27) or higher
  • Approximately 13 MB of free storage space

Step 1: Download the APK

Visit the VoxPlan Releases page on GitHub.

Download the latest VoxPlan-X.X-MVP.apk file to your Android device.

Tip: You can also browse all previous versions at github.com/richardmthompson/VoxPlanApp/releases

Step 2: Enable Installation from Unknown Sources

Since VoxPlan is installed directly (not from Google Play Store), you'll need to allow your device to install apps from unknown sources:

  1. Open Settings on your Android device
  2. Navigate to Security or Privacy
  3. Find Install unknown apps (on newer Android versions) or Unknown sources (on older versions)
  4. Enable installation for your browser or file manager app
Note: The exact location of this setting varies by Android version and device manufacturer. On Android 8+, you grant permission per-app. On older versions, it's a system-wide toggle.

Step 3: Install the APK

  1. Open your device's Downloads folder or notification panel
  2. Tap the downloaded VoxPlan-X.X-MVP.apk file
  3. Review the app permissions
  4. Tap Install
  5. Wait for installation to complete
  6. Tap Open to launch VoxPlan

Step 4: Launch VoxPlan

Once installed, you'll find VoxPlan in your app drawer. Tap the icon to launch and start organizing your goals!

Updating VoxPlan

To update to a newer version:

  1. Download the latest APK from the releases page
  2. Install the new APK over your existing installation
  3. Your goals, quotas, and progress data will be preserved
Data Safety: VoxPlan stores all data locally on your device. No internet connection required. Your goals and progress are private and secure.

Troubleshooting

Installation blocked? Make sure you've enabled "Install unknown apps" for your browser/file manager.

App not compatible? Check that your Android version is 8.1 or higher.

Need help? Report issues on GitHub

Getting Started

VoxPlan works in a simple cycle:

  1. Goals — Create and organize what you want to achieve
  2. Quotas — Set how much time you'll commit each day
  3. Focus — Work in timed sessions, earn medals
  4. Progress — Track your weekly achievements

The app has two main screens accessible from the bottom navigation: Goals and Progress.

Goals Screen

The Goals screen is your home base for organizing everything you want to accomplish.

Creating a Goal

  1. Tap the text input bar at the bottom of the screen
  2. Type your goal title
  3. Tap the add button

Your goal appears in the list, ready to work on.

Organizing with Hierarchy

Goals can be nested up to 3 levels deep:

Learn Guitar (Level 1)
├── Master Chord Progressions (Level 2)
│   ├── Practice G-C-D progression (Level 3)
│   └── Learn F chord fingering (Level 3)
└── Learn to Read Tablature (Level 2)

To view subgoals: Tap the expand arrow on any goal with children.

To navigate into a goal: Tap the list icon to drill down. A breadcrumb trail appears at the top showing your path back.

To return: Tap any breadcrumb to jump back up the hierarchy.

Example of a 3-level goal hierarchy with expanded subgoals

Reordering Goals

Use the four arrow buttons in the toolbar:

Button Action
↑ Move goal up in list
↓ Move goal down in list
← Promote to parent level
→ Demote to child of goal above

How to reorder:

  1. Tap an arrow button (it highlights)
  2. Tap the goal you want to move
  3. The goal moves according to your selection

Arrow buttons in the toolbar for reordering goals

Editing a Goal

  1. Tap a goal to reveal the action menu
  2. Tap the edit icon (pencil)
  3. Modify the title, add a quota, or adjust settings
  4. Save your changes

Deleting a Goal

  1. Tap a goal to reveal the action menu
  2. Tap the delete icon (trash)
  3. Confirm deletion
Note: Deleting a goal also removes all its subgoals.

Setting Quotas

Quotas define how much time you commit to spending on a goal each day. They're the bridge between "I want to do this" and "I'm actually doing this."

Adding a Quota

  1. Tap a goal to edit it
  2. Find the Quota section
  3. Set your daily minutes (e.g., 60 minutes)
  4. Select active days (e.g., Monday through Friday)
  5. Save

Active Days Pattern

Choose which days your quota applies:

  • Weekdays: Mon-Fri (common for work/study goals)
  • Weekends: Sat-Sun (hobbies, personal projects)
  • Every day: All seven days
  • Custom: Any combination you need

How Quotas Help

  • Goals with quotas appear in the Progress screen
  • You can see at a glance whether you're meeting your commitments
  • Quotas give structure without rigidity—they're targets, not chains

Focus Mode

Focus Mode transforms your phone into a dedicated work timer with gamified rewards.

Entering Focus Mode

Tap the stream icon on any goal or subgoal. This launches Focus Mode with that goal selected.

Focus Mode showing active timer and medal progress

Timer Controls

  • Start: Begin your focus session
  • Pause: Temporarily stop (preserves your progress)
  • Stop: End the session

Earning Medals

You earn a medal for every 30 minutes of focused work:

Time Reward
30 min 1 medal
60 min 2 medals
90 min 3 medals
... ...

Earning Crystals

Achieve a crystal when you log 4 or more hours of focus in a single day. Crystals represent exceptional dedication.

The Power Bar

The power bar at the top of the Goals screen shows your daily progress:

  • Each bar segment = 60 minutes of work
  • Fill 4 bars = earn a diamond (displayed below "POWER:")
  • The gold coin shows remaining minutes toward the next bar

Power bar displaying filled segments and progress toward next bar

Banking Your Time

After a focus session:

  1. Review your earned time and medals
  2. Tap Bank Time to save your progress
  3. Your time is recorded against that goal for today

Banked time appears in the Progress screen and counts toward your quota.

Progress Screen

The Progress screen shows your weekly achievements at a glance.

Weekly View

Each day of the week shows:

  • Which goals you worked on
  • How much time you logged
  • Whether you met your quota

Weekly progress view with quota indicators and achievements

Reading the Indicators

Stars represent full hours of quota:

  • Gold star = hour achieved
  • Grey star = hour not yet achieved

Circles represent half-hours (for quotas like 30, 90, 150 minutes):

  • Gold circle = half-hour achieved
  • Grey circle = half-hour not yet achieved

Purple indicators show overachievement:

  • Purple star = extra hour beyond quota
  • Purple circle = extra half-hour beyond quota

Example

A goal with a 90-minute quota shows: ★ ● (1 star + 1 circle)

If you worked 2 hours: ★ ● ★ (gold star, gold circle, purple star for the extra 30 min)

Navigating Weeks

Use the arrow buttons next to the date range to view previous or upcoming weeks.

Actual Time Display

Each goal row shows the actual time logged in hours and minutes (e.g., "1h 30m"), giving you precise tracking beyond the visual indicators.

Tips & Workflows

Suggested Daily Workflow

  1. Morning: Review your goals and quotas
  2. Work sessions: Enter Focus Mode, work in 30+ minute blocks
  3. Bank time: After each session, bank your progress
  4. Evening: Check Progress screen to see your day's achievements

Best Practices for Goal Hierarchy

  • Level 1: Big-picture goals (Learn Guitar, Get Fit, Build App)
  • Level 2: Major milestones or categories
  • Level 3: Specific, actionable tasks

Keep it simple. If you need more than 3 levels, your Level 1 goal might be too broad—consider splitting it.

Quota Tips

  • Start small: 30 minutes daily is better than 2 hours you'll never do
  • Be realistic about active days: Don't set 7 days if you know weekends are busy
  • Adjust as needed: Quotas aren't permanent—tune them based on what works

Focus Mode Tips

  • Minimize distractions: Close other apps, silence notifications
  • Commit to 30 minutes: The first medal is the hardest—push through
  • Take breaks: After banking time, step away before the next session
  • Celebrate crystals: 4+ hours in a day is a real achievement
